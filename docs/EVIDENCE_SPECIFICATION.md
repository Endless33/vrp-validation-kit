# Evidence Specification

## Purpose

This document defines the structure and engineering principles of validation evidence generated by the VRP Validation Kit.

Evidence exists to make engineering claims independently verifiable.

Engineering conclusions should be based on observable artifacts rather than verbal statements.

---

# Design Goals

Evidence should be:

- reproducible

- deterministic

- auditable

- portable

- independently verifiable

- suitable for long-term storage

---

# Evidence Components

A validation run may generate one or more of the following artifacts.

## Validation Report

Human-readable engineering summary describing:

- validation scenario

- execution environment

- observed behavior

- validation verdict

---

## Evidence Bundle

Machine-readable collection of validation artifacts.

May include:

- reports

- manifests

- hashes

- signatures

- runtime metadata

---

## Manifest

The manifest defines the contents of an evidence bundle.

Typical fields include:

- validation identifier

- generation timestamp

- bundle version

- artifact list

- integrity hashes

---

## Hashes

Hashes provide integrity verification.

Hash values should be generated after evidence creation.

Any modification should produce a different hash.

---

## Signatures

When supported,

evidence may include digital signatures.

Signatures allow an evaluator to verify evidence authenticity.

---

# Engineering Properties

Evidence should satisfy the following properties.

## Integrity

Evidence should reveal modification attempts.

---

## Reproducibility

Equivalent validation procedures should generate equivalent engineering conclusions.

---

## Auditability

Evidence should allow independent review after validation has completed.

---

## Portability

Evidence should remain usable across supported operating systems.

---

## Independence

Evidence should be understandable without requiring protected runtime implementation.

---

# Validation Lifecycle

Typical evidence lifecycle:

Validation Execution

↓

Evidence Generation

↓

Integrity Verification

↓

Independent Review

↓

Engineering Decision

---

# Long-Term Preservation

Evidence should remain suitable for future engineering review.

Historical validation artifacts should remain distinguishable from future validation artifacts.

Published reports should reference the corresponding evidence bundle whenever possible.

---

# Engineering Philosophy

Evidence is part of the engineering process.

Engineering work that cannot be independently reviewed is significantly harder to evaluate.

The Validation Kit therefore treats evidence generation as a first-class engineering component.

---

# Final Principle

Claims should be supported by evidence.

Evidence should be reproducible.

Reproducibility creates confidence.

Confidence supports engineering decisions.
