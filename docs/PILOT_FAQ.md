# Private Pilot FAQ

## Purpose

This document answers the most common engineering questions regarding the VRP Private Pilot Program.

The goal is to help engineering teams, architects, technical managers, and decision makers understand what the Pilot is intended to evaluate.

---

# Q1

## Why is the runtime not open source?

Because the objective of the Pilot is engineering evaluation rather than source code disclosure.

The protected runtime contains proprietary implementation and commercial intellectual property.

The Validation Kit allows independent engineering validation without requiring unrestricted access to protected implementation.

---

# Q2

## Can we verify the technology without the protected source code?

Yes.

The Validation Kit exists specifically for that purpose.

Organizations are encouraged to:

- download the Validation Kit

- execute the published validation procedures

- review generated evidence

- compare reproduced results with published reports

Observable engineering behavior is the primary evaluation target.

---

# Q3

## What additional value does the Private Pilot provide?

The Pilot evaluates the protected runtime under controlled engineering conditions.

It allows organizations to assess operational behavior that cannot be demonstrated through the public validation layer alone.

---

# Q4

## Does the Pilot disclose proprietary algorithms?

No.

The purpose of the Pilot is evaluation.

Not technology transfer.

Protected implementation remains protected.

---

# Q5

## Can we modify the protected runtime?

No.

The protected runtime is evaluated as provided within the Pilot framework.

Observable behavior is evaluated.

Protected implementation is not redistributed.

---

# Q6

## Can our engineers perform independent testing?

Yes.

Independent engineering evaluation is encouraged.

Participants are expected to:

- execute validation scenarios

- review generated evidence

- compare observed behavior

- reproduce published procedures

Engineering verification is encouraged throughout the Pilot.

---

# Q7

## What should we prepare before requesting a Pilot?

Organizations should identify:

- engineering objectives

- evaluation environment

- technical contacts

- success criteria

- expected deployment scenario

Clear objectives improve the quality of the Pilot.

---

# Q8

## How long does a Pilot usually last?

The duration depends on the agreed evaluation scope.

Larger environments generally require more extensive validation.

The objective is engineering confidence rather than speed.

---

# Q9

## What happens after the Pilot?

The organization reviews the engineering evidence and determines whether the protected runtime satisfies its operational requirements.

The Pilot concludes with an engineering decision.

---

# Q10

## What if the Validation Kit already satisfies our evaluation?

Then the organization may determine that additional evaluation is unnecessary.

The Private Pilot exists for organizations requiring deeper operational assessment of the protected runtime.

Participation is optional.

---

# Engineering Philosophy

The objective is not to convince anyone.

The objective is to provide sufficient engineering evidence for informed technical decisions.

---

# Final Principle

Do not trust claims.

Validate them.

Do not assume behavior.

Observe it.

Do not rely on promises.

Make engineering decisions based on independently reproducible evidence.
