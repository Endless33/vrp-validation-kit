# VRP Open Engineering Challenge

## Purpose

This challenge is intended for engineers, security researchers, protocol designers, and organizations that wish to independently evaluate the published VRP validation environment.

The objective is not to debate opinions.

The objective is to produce reproducible engineering evidence.

---

# Engineering Principle

Do not trust marketing.

Do not trust assumptions.

Do not trust opinions.

Trust reproducible technical evidence.

---

# Challenge Scope

A successful challenge must demonstrate a reproducible violation of one or more documented VRP engineering invariants.

Examples include, but are not limited to:

- Session continuity violation
- Replay acceptance
- Authority bypass
- Recovery invariant failure
- Deterministic evidence inconsistency
- Canonical evidence forgery
- State corruption
- Fail-Closed violation
- Documented security invariant violation

The reported issue must be:

- reproducible;
- independently verifiable;
- accompanied by complete technical evidence.

---

# What Does NOT Count

The following do not constitute a successful challenge:

- personal opinions;
- theoretical speculation;
- unsupported claims;
- unverifiable assumptions;
- implementation preferences;
- hypothetical attack scenarios without reproducible evidence.

---

# Required Submission

A valid submission must include:

- complete reproduction steps;
- environment information;
- logs;
- configuration;
- evidence bundle;
- expected behavior;
- observed behavior;
- explanation of the violated invariant.

---

# If a Valid Issue Is Found

If a participant demonstrates a reproducible violation of a documented VRP invariant:

- the finding will be publicly acknowledged;
- the researcher will receive full engineering credit (if desired);
- the issue will be investigated and addressed;
- the participant will be permanently listed in the VRP Engineering Hall of Fame.

---

# If No Violation Can Be Reproduced

If participants are unable to demonstrate a reproducible violation of the documented engineering invariants within the published challenge scope, the appropriate engineering conclusion is simply:

"The documented challenge did not produce reproducible evidence of an engineering invariant violation."

No stronger conclusion should be drawn.

Engineering is an ongoing process, and future testing is always encouraged.

---

# Hall of Fame

Researchers who contribute verified findings improve the engineering quality of VRP.

Constructive criticism supported by reproducible evidence is always welcome.

---

# Final Principle

Engineering progresses through evidence.

Not through assumptions.

Not through reputation.

Not through marketing.

Only through results that others can independently reproduce.

— Vitalijus Riabovas
