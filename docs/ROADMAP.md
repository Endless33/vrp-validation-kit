# Public Validation Roadmap

## Purpose

This document describes the planned evolution of the VRP Validation Kit.

The roadmap reflects engineering direction rather than delivery guarantees.

Development priorities may change as validation experience grows.

---

# Current Focus

The current focus is to improve:

- reproducibility

- validation coverage

- engineering documentation

- evidence quality

- independent verification

- long-duration validation

---

# Near-Term Objectives

The following improvements are planned as engineering priorities.

## Expanded Validation Scenarios

Increase the number of publicly reproducible validation scenarios.

Examples include:

- transport migration

- recovery sequences

- long-duration execution

- degraded network conditions

- evidence validation

---

## Additional Evidence

Expand generated engineering artifacts.

Future releases may include:

- richer reports

- expanded manifests

- additional evidence metadata

- improved validation summaries

---

## Documentation

Continue improving public engineering documentation.

Future additions may include:

- architecture notes

- deployment examples

- engineering references

- operational guidance

---

## Cross-Platform Validation

Expand validation coverage across supported operating systems.

Engineering conclusions should remain reproducible regardless of supported execution platform.

---

## Independent Evaluation

Encourage additional independent engineering validation.

Engineering confidence increases when equivalent conclusions are obtained by unrelated evaluators.

---

# Long-Term Direction

Future engineering work may explore:

- larger validation matrices

- extended runtime validation

- additional deployment environments

- expanded evidence formats

- improved validation automation

- additional engineering documentation

---

# Out of Scope

This roadmap does not describe:

- protected runtime implementation

- proprietary algorithms

- commercial licensing strategy

- confidential development plans

Those subjects remain outside the public validation boundary.

---

# Engineering Philosophy

The Validation Kit is intended to mature gradually.

Every release should improve engineering confidence through observable progress.

The roadmap should remain stable, understandable, and focused on reproducible validation.

---

# Final Statement

The objective is not simply to add more features.

The objective is to improve the quality, reproducibility, and engineering value of every validation release.
